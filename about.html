About me

I am a postdoctoral researcher and teaching assistant in the Analysis Institut of the Leibniz Universität Hannover (LUH), which is currently directed by Prof. Alexander Strohmaier.

Before that, I completed my PhD in Mathematics (Dr. rer. nat.) within the Analysis group in the Institute for Mathematics of the Carl von Ossietzky Universität Oldenburg under the supervision of Prof. Boris Vertman and Prof. Daniel Grieser. My thesis was titled Index theorem on fibred boundary manifolds.

My main research interests belong to the field of geometric microlocal analysis, which lies at the intersection of differential geometry, global analysis and partial differential equations. One of the main goals is to understand to what extent certain operators on a manifold reflect its geometry. The archetypical example is spectral geometry, which tries to determine what geometric information on the manifold is determined by the spectrum of its Laplacian. Paraphrasing Marc Kac: What features of the shape of a space can one hear? Great milestones in the theory were the Atiyah-Singer and Atiyah-Patodi-Singer index theorems, which connect topological and geometrical magnitudes with spectral properties of Dirac operators. My focus is on singular geometries, i.e. either complete manifolds with non-compact ends that "are very far away" (like an infinite cylinder) or stratified manifolds with associated incomplete metrics (such as a polyhedron).

My MSc in Mathematics was in the Rheinische Friedrich Wilhelms Universität Bonn. My Master thesis was supervised by Prof. Ursula Hamenstädt and Dr. Alexander Thomas and titled Higher Teichmüller theory: Barycenter parametrization of Hitchin components. I also have a BSc in Mathematics and a BSc in Physics by the Universidad Complutense de Madrid, with theses on Teichmüller theory (Dr. Luis Giraldo Suárez) and spacetime singularities (Dr. Diego Rubiera García), respectively.
