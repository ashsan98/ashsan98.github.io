About me

I am a postdoctoral researcher and teaching assistant in the Analysis Institut of the Leibniz Universität Hannover (LUH), which is currently directed by Prof. Alexander Strohmaier.

Before that, I completed my PhD in Mathematics (Dr. rer. nat.) within the Analysis group in the Institute for Mathematics of the Carl von Ossietzky Universität Oldenburg under the supervision of Prof. Boris Vertman and Prof. Daniel Grieser. My thesis was titled Index theorem on fibred boundary manifolds.

My MSc in Mathematics was in the Rheinische Friedrich Wilhelms Universität Bonn. My Master thesis was supervised by Prof. Ursula Hamenstädt and Dr. Alexander Thomas and titled Higher Teichmüller theory: Barycenter parametrization of Hitchin components. I also have a BSc in Mathematics and a BSc in Physics by the Universidad Complutense de Madrid, with theses on Teichmüller theory (Dr. Luis Giraldo Suárez) and spacetime singularities (Dr. Diego Rubiera García), respectively.
