Research

My main research interests belong to the field of geometric microlocal analysis, which lies at the intersection of differential geometry, global analysis and partial differential equations. One of the main goals is to understand to what extent certain operators on a manifold reflect its geometry. The archetypical example is spectral geometry, which tries to determine what geometric information on the manifold is determined by the spectrum of its Laplacian. Paraphrasing Marc Kac: What features of the shape of a space can one hear? Great milestones in the theory were the Atiyah-Singer and Atiyah-Patodi-Singer index theorems, which connect topological and geometrical magnitudes with spectral properties of Dirac operators. My focus is on singular geometries, i.e. either complete manifolds with non-compact ends that "are very far away" (like an infinite cylinder) or stratified manifolds with associated incomplete metrics (such as a polyhedron).
